Professional Life

I graduated college in 2002 with bachelor's degrees in Analytical Chemistry and Structural Geology. During my time at college, I was the chemistry department computer lab administrator, Geology club treasurer, and a lab tech/intern at the BLM water resource lab in Alamosa, CO. Shortly after graduation I started my professional career as an analytical chemist and a geologist performing analysis and characterizations. This allowed for a variety of experiences in both lab and field settings.

In the lab I was able to properly operate, calibrate and maintain a number of analytical instruments. These devices include HPLC, GC-NMR, UV-Vis, XRF, and ISOX to name a few. This gave me a wide variety of experiences allowing me to apply my academics in the real world. This lab work was in a variety of settings from government and private production labs to working in R&D. This gave me an appreciation of accuracy and consistency of technique and operational standards.

In the field I performed a number of tasks from sampling, core logging, and materials testing. To perform these duties I used additional analytical equipment such as flow meters, pressure sensors, gas/air monitoring equipment and other manual methods. Hammer drill and explosives was a highlight. These operations were mostly in the mineral or construction side of the world of geology world. Here I learned that even tighter controls needed to be adhered to prevent cross contamination so a good lab test could occur.

During this time, I learned a talent for instrumentation and programming on a variety of softwares and platforms. I also applied my academic programming skills to create simple scripts and programs to make my day easier. This inspired me to return to school in 2009 for engineering with an emphasis in electronics and instrumentation. After graduation 2013 I began my career in Electrical Designer and Controls System Engineering world. This allowed me to tie my instrumentation background into a new operational space.

In this role I have designed, repaired, troubleshot, and retrofitted several types of control systems. These systems can be found in the mineral extraction, water/waste-water, manufacturing and in various facets of oil and gas. Some of these systems varied in complexity from standalone units to cellular WAN connected fields.

As part of this I have worked with many different PLC ecosystems, mixed and unified, paired with both Windows and Linux based environments. This variety of PLC hardware includes Allen-Bradly, Siemens, ABB, OPTO22 and a variety of others manufactures. I have programmed these units in structured text, function block, and ladder logic based on need, operation and user preference. To add to this I have created a number of AOI's and data structures to allow simplification and reuse of code.

Some of these systems have had server based SCADA system overlays, simple HMI controls, regional DCS style controls, and headless units that use a custom web based interfaces. This variety of HMI softwares include FactoryTalk, S7, Ignition, ABB Graphics, and NodeRed to start the list. One such endeavor including a system involving edge/IOT computing to allow the remote monitoring and operations while using the dependability of a PLC to control an oil and gas well site, tank emission control systems (TEMS). This was performed in several layers using a Java script head, Python and C++ inter device communications and structured text PLC code.

Personal Life

Although lately I have mostly been doing projects with my kids and the home improvement gig as the schedule allows.

Not that my list is not growing, current count 35, just the physical manifestation of that list.
